## Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into transferring money through fake requests or links. This crime usually begins when a victim receives a call, message, or QR code from someone pretending to be a bank official, seller, or customer support agent.

The process typically happens in steps. First, the fraudster gains the victim’s trust by creating urgency (e.g., “your account will be blocked” or “you will receive a refund”). Next, they send a payment request or QR code and instruct the victim to approve it. Many users mistakenly believe they are receiving money, but in reality, they are authorizing a payment. Once the victim enters their UPI PIN, the money is instantly transferred to the fraudster’s account.

College students and young users are often targeted because they frequently use UPI apps and may not fully understand how payment requests work. The consequences include immediate financial loss, stress, and difficulty recovering the stolen money. In some cases, victims also share personal details, which can lead to further fraud.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and careful verification before approving any online transaction.
